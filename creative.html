The New Bazaar

What moves here is less than touch,
not a hand outstretched
but a pause before a swipe,
or a glance away,
a message typed, revised, abandoned,
every click tracked.

Lobed fruit—

pulp split.

Pine-Sol, car cleaner.

Shards of summer.

Artemis II

And when you meet that unseen patch of moon,
would it feel like discovery?
Or would it feel like coming home
to a radiant absence,
as if you had been missing it without knowing:
Earth's bisected half,
broken from our planet by ancient collision,
drifting into space while strangled in our orbit?

What was it like when we were one body?
sailing across the Sea of Serenity,
skipping stones across the Orientale Basin four billion years ago
